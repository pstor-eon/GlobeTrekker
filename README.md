### Ride Hailing Platforms
**Uber** : Available in over 60 countries and 900 metropolitan areas worldwide, Uber is probably the most recognized ride-hailing app. In addition to standard car rides, Uber also offers services like food delivery (Uber Eats), freight transportation (Uber Freight), and even helicopter rides in some areas.

**Lyft** : Uber's main competitor in the United States. They operate in hundreds of cities across the U.S. and in select cities in Canada.

**Didi Chuxing** : Often referred to as "China's Uber," Didi is the leading ride-hailing service in China. They also have operations in a handful of other countries including Australia, Japan, and several countries in Latin America.

**Grab** : Based in Singapore, Grab operates in Southeast Asia and offers a variety of services beyond just ride-hailing, including food delivery and digital payments.

**Ola** : Ola is India's counterpart to Uber and operates in over 100 cities across India, Australia, New Zealand, and the UK.

**Kakao T** : Kakao T (previously KakaoTaxi) is a major ride-hailing app in South Korea. It's operated by Kakao Corporation, the same company that runs the popular KakaoTalk messaging app.

**Gojek and Grab** : 
hese are popular in Indonesia and other Southeast Asian countries, offering ride-hailing, delivery, and various on-demand services.

**Bolt (formerly Taxify)** : Bolt is a popular ride-hailing app in several European and African countries.

**Yandex Go** : Yandex Go, previously Yandex.Taxi, is a popular ride-hailing service in Russia and some surrounding countries.

**Careem** : Careem operates in over 100 cities in the Middle East, Africa, and South Asia. It was acquired by Uber in 2019 but continues to operate under its own brand.

**BlaBlaCar** : BlaBlaCar is a long-distance carpooling service popular in Europe.

**Cabify** : Cabify is a Spanish ride-hailing company that operates in various countries in Latin America, as well as Spain and Portugal.

### Bike Sharing Platforms
**Kakao T Bike** : In South Korea, Kakao provides a bike-sharing platform under its Kakao T services. Users can use the app to locate and rent bikes.

**Mobike** : This is a Chinese bike-sharing company that operates in several countries worldwide.

**Lime and Bird** : These companies started with electric scooter sharing, but they also offer shared bicycles in some markets. They operate in several countries around the world.

**Citi Bike** : This is a bike-sharing program in New York City. Users can rent bikes from docking stations scattered throughout the city.

**Santander Cycles** : Known locally as "Boris Bikes", this is a public bicycle hire scheme in London, UK.

### Car Sharing Platforms
**SoCar** : SoCar is a South Korean car-sharing company that allows you to rent cars by the hour. The app allows users to find and book vehicles, which are often conveniently located in residential and commercial areas.

**Green Car** : Green Car is another car-sharing service in South Korea. They operate similarly to SoCar, with cars available for hourly rental.

**Zipcar** : This is one of the largest car-sharing networks in the world, with operations in urban areas and college campuses throughout the United States, Canada, and Europe.

**Car2Go** : Operated by Daimler AG, Car2Go provides car-sharing services in several European and North American cities.

**DriveNow** : This is a car-sharing service operated by BMW and Sixt in several European cities.

**Getaround and Turo** : These are peer-to-peer car-sharing services, meaning the cars are owned by individuals rather than the company. Car owners can list their vehicles on the platform to rent out when they're not using them.
